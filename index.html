About Kerala

Kerala is a state on the southwestern Malabar Coast of India. It is known for its palm-lined beaches, serene backwaters, network of canals, and rich cultural heritage.

Natural Beauty

From the tranquil backwaters to the rolling hills of tea plantations, Kerala offers some of the most stunning natural landscapes in India. You can experience serene beaches, exotic wildlife, and refreshing waterfalls.

  • Backwaters of Alleppey
  • Tea Gardens of Munnar
  • Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Varkala and Kovalam Beaches

Cultural Heritage

Kerala has a rich cultural heritage with its classical dance forms, music, festivals, and temples. Famous for Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, the culture of Kerala reflects its deep connection with nature and spirituality.

  • Kathakali Dance
  • Onam Festival
  • Ayurvedic Traditions

Tourist Attractions

Kerala is home to many unique attractions that draw travelers from all over the world.

Alleppey Backwaters

Enjoy houseboat rides through serene waters surrounded by lush greenery.

Munnar

Explore the tea plantations and misty hills in the cool climate of Munnar.

Thekkady

Visit the Periyar National Park for a unique experience of wildlife in Kerala.
